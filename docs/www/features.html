Reverse Engineering

Reverse engineering eliminates the need to design from a blank sheet. The process takes an existing part, captures its surface measurements, and uses this information as a reference to create the new design. Reverse engineering techniques can contribute to solving the following tasks:
  • Create CAD models for legacy parts where no digital models are available.
  • Design organic shapes whose features are very difficult to reproduce without a polygonal underlayer.
  • Change (optimize) design of existing product.
  • Replicate existing products.
  • Repair worn-out or broken parts.
  • Understand better the properties of the existing parts (e.g. it symmetry, defects, etc.).
  • Convert models to editable format after topologic optimization.
  • Convert triangular and quadrilateral surface meshes to higher-order models for isogeometric analysis.
